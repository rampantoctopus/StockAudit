THE PROBLEM

Your download folder and your final cut tell two different stories.

You downloaded forty clips over three weeks of editing. Twelve made it into the final timeline. Which twelve, and how many times did each one actually get used?

At delivery, someone always asks for a stock license report — and by then, reconstructing exactly what got used, how often, and for how long is a guessing game nobody has time for.

HOW IT WORKS

Three steps, no manual cross-checking.

Everything runs locally on your machine. Nothing uploads anywhere.

01

Export your timeline

Export an XMEML sequence from Premiere Pro — the same export you already do for conforms.

02

Point it at your download log

Drop in your StockSilo log, or any CSV/XLSX with clip names, source sites, and download dates.

03

Get a licensing-ready workbook

StockAudit matches clips by name, tallies usage and duration, and flags anything that looks like stock but wasn't logged.

WHAT YOU GET

One workbook, four tabs, everything a licensing report needs.

Sheet 01

Stock Footage Used

Every matched clip with source site, times used, and duration on the timeline.

Sheet 02

Music Cue Sheet

Same treatment for music and audio cues — source, usage count, duration.

Sheet 03 · flagged

Non-Stock Media

Everything else, with anything that looks like unlogged stock called out.

Sheet 04

Summary

Totals, sequence frame rate, and the exact options used to generate the report.
